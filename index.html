Our Two-Phase Resolution Process

1

Phase 1: Investigation & Representation

The first step in resolving your tax issues is a thorough and meticulous investigation of your unique situation. We begin by conducting a comprehensive assessment of your financial records, tax history, and communications with the IRS.

  • Client Assessment: Understanding your financial standing, income, assets, and liabilities.
  • Documentation Review: Gathering and scrutinizing all relevant tax documents, notices, and correspondence.
  • IRS Communication & Representation: We act as your authorized representative, handling all direct communication with the IRS on your behalf, protecting you from stressful interactions and ensuring your rights are upheld.
2

Phase 2: Resolution & Negotiation

Once we have a complete understanding of your case, we move to actively negotiate with the IRS to achieve the most favorable outcome possible. Our expertise in the Fresh Start Initiative allows us to explore various relief options.

  • Offer in Compromise (OIC): Negotiating to settle your tax debt for a lower amount than what you owe.
  • Installment Agreements: Establishing manageable monthly payment plans.
  • Penalty Abatement: Requesting the removal of penalties due to reasonable cause.
  • Currently Non-Collectible (CNC): If you cannot afford to pay, we can help you achieve CNC status.
  • Lien & Levy Release: Working to remove federal tax liens and levies.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the tax resolution process typically take?

The timeline varies depending on your specific situation and chosen resolution option. Most clients see initial relief within 2-4 weeks. For complex cases involving Offer in Compromise, the process may take 2-6 months. We'll provide you with a realistic timeline during your initial consultation.

What are your fees, and how do you structure payments?

Our fees depend on the complexity of your case and the resolution option we pursue. We offer flexible payment arrangements and will discuss all costs upfront before you commit to our services. Many clients find our fees are quickly recouped through the tax savings we negotiate.

What's your success rate? Will the IRS approve my case?

Our success rate is over 90% across all resolution options. While we can't guarantee IRS approval (they make the final decision), our expertise in presentation and negotiation significantly increases your chances. We'll assess your situation and only recommend options we believe have strong approval potential.

Do I have to pay the IRS while working with you?

Typically, no. Once you engage our services and we become your authorized representative, IRS collection activities usually pause while we negotiate. This gives you breathing room and makes our job easier. We'll discuss payment expectations for any settlement we arrange.

Can you help if I haven't filed my tax returns?

Yes, absolutely. Filing back taxes is often the first step in our resolution process. We can guide you through this and work with you to prepare unfiled returns. Once filed, we can then pursue the appropriate relief option for your situation.

What information do I need to bring to our first consultation?

Please bring any IRS notices you've received, recent tax returns (if available), and a summary of your income and expenses. Our Tax Resolution Checklist (available in our Resources section) has a complete list of what helps us assess your situation quickly.

Is my information confidential?

Yes. As enrolled agents, we're bound by strict confidentiality rules. Your tax information is protected, and we only share what's necessary with the IRS to negotiate on your behalf. Our privacy policy details how we handle your information.
