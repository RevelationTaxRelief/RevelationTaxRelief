Penalty Abatement: Grounds and Procedures

Published: November 23, 2025

The IRS assesses various penalties for non-compliance with tax laws, including penalties for failure to file, failure to pay, and accuracy-related issues. These penalties can significantly increase your overall tax burden. However, in certain circumstances, the IRS may agree to remove or "abate" these penalties if you can demonstrate a valid reason for non-compliance.

Grounds for Penalty Abatement

The IRS generally considers three main grounds for penalty abatement:

  • Reasonable Cause: This is the most common ground. You must show that you exercised ordinary business care and prudence but were still unable to comply with your tax obligations. Examples include serious illness, death in the immediate family, natural disaster, or unavoidable absence.
  • First-Time Abate (FTA): If you have a clean compliance history for the past three years, the IRS may abate failure-to-file and failure-to-pay penalties for a single tax period. You must have filed all required returns and paid, or arranged to pay, any tax due.
  • Statutory Exception: Certain penalties may be abated if they are due to incorrect written advice from the IRS, or if the penalty is related to an issue for which the IRS has specific statutory relief.

The Abatement Procedure

Requesting penalty abatement involves a formal process:

  1. Identify Penalties: Understand which penalties have been assessed and for which tax periods.
  2. Determine Grounds: Evaluate if your situation qualifies under reasonable cause, FTA, or a statutory exception.
  3. Gather Documentation: Collect all supporting evidence that substantiates your claim (e.g., medical records, disaster declarations, correspondence).
  4. Submit Request: File a written request (often Form 843, Claim for Refund and Request for Abatement, or a letter) with the IRS, clearly explaining your reasons and attaching all documentation.
  5. Follow-Up: Monitor the status of your request and be prepared to provide additional information if needed.
