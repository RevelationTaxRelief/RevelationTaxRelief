Understanding IRS Audits: A Guide for Taxpayers

Published: October 26, 2025

Receiving a notice from the IRS indicating an audit can be a source of significant anxiety. An IRS audit is a review or examination of an individual's or organization's accounts and financial information to ensure information is reported correctly according to tax laws and to verify the amount of tax reported is accurate.

What Triggers an Audit?

Audits can be triggered by various factors, including discrepancies between reported income and third-party statements (like W-2s or 1099s), unusually high deductions, significant changes in income from previous years, or simply random selection. While some audits are purely statistical, others arise from specific red flags in your tax return.

Types of Audits

The IRS conducts different types of audits:

  • Correspondence Audits: These are the most common and are conducted entirely by mail. The IRS requests specific documents or information to clarify an item on your return.
  • Office Audits: These require you to visit a local IRS office with your records. They typically involve more complex issues than correspondence audits.
  • Field Audits: The most comprehensive type, where an IRS agent visits your home, place of business, or your accountant's office to examine your financial records.
