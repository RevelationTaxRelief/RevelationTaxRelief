Navigating Back Taxes: Options and Ethical Considerations

Published: November 2, 2025

Owing back taxes can be a daunting situation, often accompanied by stress and uncertainty about the future. It's crucial to understand that ignoring the problem will only lead to more severe penalties and enforcement actions from tax authorities. Proactive and ethical engagement is key to finding a resolution.

Common Back Tax Resolution Options

The IRS and state tax agencies offer several programs for taxpayers who owe back taxes but cannot pay immediately:

  • Installment Agreement (IA): Allows taxpayers to make monthly payments for up to 72 months. This is a common solution for those who can pay their full tax liability over time.
  • Offer in Compromise (OIC): Allows certain taxpayers to resolve their tax liability with the IRS for a lower amount than what they originally owe. An OIC is typically granted when there is doubt as to collectibility, liability, or effective tax administration.
  • Currently Not Collectible (CNC): If the IRS determines you cannot pay your tax debt due to financial hardship, they may temporarily delay collection until your financial situation improves.
  • Penalty Abatement: In certain circumstances, the IRS may remove penalties if you can demonstrate reasonable cause for non-compliance.
