Preparing for Your Initial Tax Consultation: What to Expect

Published: December 28, 2025

Your initial consultation with Revelation Tax Relief is a crucial first step toward resolving your tax challenges. It's an opportunity for us to understand your situation thoroughly and for you to learn about your options. To make this meeting as productive and efficient as possible, coming prepared with certain information and questions can be highly beneficial.

Documents to Bring (If Available)

While we can often obtain records directly from tax authorities with your authorization, having these documents readily available can expedite the assessment process:

  • IRS Notices: Any letters or notices you've received from the IRS or state tax agencies (e.g., CP2000, CP504, Notice of Intent to Levy, Audit notices).
  • Tax Returns: Copies of your filed tax returns for the years in question.
  • Income Documentation: W-2s, 1099s, K-1s, or other statements of income for relevant years.
  • Financial Statements: Recent bank statements, investment statements, and a list of assets (e.g., real estate, vehicles) and liabilities (e.g., mortgages, loans).
  • Proof of Expenses: Documentation for significant deductions or credits claimed (e.g., medical expenses, business expenses, charitable contributions).
  • Identification: A valid photo ID and Social Security card.

Questions to Consider Asking

Preparing a list of questions can help ensure all your concerns are addressed during the consultation:

  • What are my primary tax issues, and what are the potential consequences if unresolved?
  • What are the most viable resolution options for my specific situation?
  • What is your recommended strategy, and why?
  • What is the estimated timeline for resolving my case?
  • What are your fees, and how are they structured?
  • What information and cooperation will you require from me?
  • How will you communicate updates regarding my case?
  • What steps can I take to prevent similar tax issues in the future?

What to Expect During the Consultation

During your initial consultation, a Revelation Tax Relief professional will:

  • Listen attentively to your concerns and gather details about your tax history.
  • Review any documents you've provided.
  • Provide an initial assessment of your situation and explain potential paths to resolution.
  • Discuss our ethical approach, transparent process, and how we can assist you.
  • Answer your questions thoroughly and clearly.
  • Outline the next steps if you choose to engage our services.

Disclaimer: The initial consultation provides an assessment of your situation. Specific outcomes cannot be guaranteed, as all final determinations are made by tax authorities and depend on the specifics of each case.
